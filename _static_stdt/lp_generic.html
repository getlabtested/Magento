Could I have Chlamydia?

You may be surprised to learn how many people have Chlamydia, the most common STD in the United States. In 2008 alone, 1,210,523 instances of Chlamydia infection were reported. Chlamydia infection can still be present even in the absence of symptoms. Only 30% of infected women and 25% of infected men display side effects of Chlamydia, which typically include genital discharge and pain during urination.

Is Chlamydia curable?

Yes, Chlamydia is curable but must be treated early. If left undetected, Chlamydia infection can cause infertility in men and women. Who needs a Chlamydia test?

All sexually active individuals are at risk for Chlamydia. The CDC recommends annual Chlamydia testing, specifically for all sexually active women age 25 and under.
